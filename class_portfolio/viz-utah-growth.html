Visualizing Utah's Population Growth

I decided to update a tutorial focusing on distributions, which demonstrates use of scatterplots, state and menus that update the data displayed on the graph.

Brief
The scatterplot linked below shows the relationships between the age of the mother, the weight of the children born, and whether or not the mother smoked cigarettes. The dataset covers pregnancies between 1960 and 1967 among women in the San Francisco East Bay area. I've used colors to differentiate between smokers and non-smokers, and a tooltip when hovering over each invidual circle to give details about the case/observation.
